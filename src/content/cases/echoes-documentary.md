## The Brief

This one started without a brief. Our director had long been fascinated by the oral tradition of jazz education in New Orleans — how songs, techniques, and stories are passed not through conservatories, but through family kitchens and front porches. We self-funded the initial development and later received support from a cultural arts grant.

## Our Approach

We spent **six weeks** living in and around the Tremé neighborhood, building trust with three families whose musical lineage stretches back to the early 1900s. Our approach was strictly observational — no sit-down interviews, no narration. The camera is always a guest in the room.

### Structural pillars:

- **The Elder** — 78-year-old trumpeter Raymond "Big Ray" Thibodaux, who still plays every Sunday at Preservation Hall
- **The Middle** — His daughter Monique, a music teacher at a local public school, fighting to keep the jazz program funded
- **The Young** — Monique's 16-year-old son Darius, torn between jazz tradition and his own love of hip-hop production

The film cross-cuts between their daily lives, letting the music itself — rehearsals, performances, casual jams — serve as the connective tissue.

## Technical Details

- **Camera:** Sony FX6 — small enough to be unobtrusive in intimate family settings
- **Lenses:** Vintage Canon FD primes for warmth and organic flare characteristics
- **Audio:** Sennheiser MKH 8060 boom and DPA 4060 lavs — capturing the full richness of live instruments was paramount
- **Editing style:** Long takes, patient observation, minimal cuts within musical performances

## Festival Run & Reception

*Echoes* premiered at the **New Orleans Film Festival**, where it won the **Audience Award for Best Short Documentary**. It went on to screen at SXSW, Hot Docs, and Sheffield Doc/Fest.

The film helped secure renewed funding for Monique's school jazz program and has been licensed for educational use by three university music departments.

> "They didn't just film our family — they understood us. That's rare." — *Monique Thibodaux*
